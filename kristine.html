About Me...

I earned my Bachelor of Arts degree with an Honors Specialization in Visual Arts from the University of Western Ontario in London, Ontario Canada, where I was born and raised. I focused on printmaking and analog photography. From there, I moved 5 hours towards Ottawa to Kingston, Ontario where I earned my Bachelor of Education degree from Queens University. I am now finishing my thesis for my Masters of Arts in Education- Visual Arts Education, from the University of Nebraska (Graduation set for December, 2015).Since beginning my career as an art teacher, I have realized that there can never be one perfect art curriculum because art educators need to alter the materials and methods taught dependent upon the group of students; who they are, where they are in the world, what their interests are etc. Objectives within an art program will never be static.
I knew that I wanted to be an art teacher since about grade 8. Art was always something that kind of came naturally to me and that I genuinely enjoy. When I was in grades 6, I babysat and planned elaborate crafts with pre-packaged supply kits that I prepared. Even younger, in grade 3 or 4, I made charts of what my sister and I would play, in what order we would do each activity and for how long each activity would take (symbols and drawings for reference included); essentially I was making lesson plans. Other pivotal life experiences include numerous camping trips, which in retrospect, prepared me for being away from home for extended lengths of time amongst other life skills; this along with the fact that my grandparents were avid world travellers, led to my own passion for travel, and sparked my eternal sense of wanderlust.
I have now visited around 50 countries and with every country I visit, I want to see more. I believe it is our responsibility to see and experience how other people, fortunate and not so fortunate, live. I also believe life isn’t meant to be lived in one place. My multicultural- Canadian upbringing and experiences around the world have made me extremely tolerant, open-minded, curious, and eager to discover more.
